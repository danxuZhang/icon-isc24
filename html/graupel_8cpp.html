Models the dynamics of graupel, snow, and ice precipitation within a model grid.

Simulates the microphysical processes involving graupel.

This function simulates the microphysical processes affecting graupel, snow, and ice within a grid-based model of the atmosphere. It accounts for the formation, growth, and fallout of these hydrometeors through the atmospheric column. The function updates the state of each cell in terms of hydrometeor mass, flux, and related properties, considering atmospheric conditions like temperature and density.

Simulates the update of precipitation in a grid cell over a time step.

This function models the dynamics of precipitation within a grid cell, including the accumulation or reduction of precipitation mass, the flux of precipitation into the cell from the cell directly above, and the calculation of terminal velocity of precipitation particles. It takes into account the physical properties of the air and precipitation, such as air density and fall speed parameters, to update the state of precipitation in the cell and its impact on adjacent cells.

Parameters
paramsAn array of real_t containing parameters related to the fall speed of precipitation.
precipAn array of real_t where the updated precipitation values will be stored.
  • precip[0] will contain the updated specific mass of hydrometeors (how much precipitation is present).
  • precip[1] will contain the flux of precipitation into the cell from above.
  • precip[2] will contain the updated terminal velocity of the precipitation particles.
zetaThe ratio of the time step for integration (dt) to twice the vertical distance between grid cells (2dz).
vcThe correction factor for the fall speed, which is state dependent (e.g., adjusted for local atmospheric conditions).
flxThe flux of precipitation entering the cell from the cell directly above at the start of the time step.
vtThe terminal velocity of precipitation particles (assumed constant for the time step).
qThe specific mass of hydrometeors (precipitation) in the cell at the start of the time step.
q_kp1The specific mass of hydrometeors in the cell directly below at the start of the time step.
rhoThe density of air in the cell.
